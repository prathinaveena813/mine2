Core Networking Concepts

These are the most important ideas to understand before learning advanced networking.

Network

A network is a group of connected devices that communicate and share resources.

LAN

A Local Area Network connects devices in a small area like a home, lab, or office.

WAN

A Wide Area Network connects devices over large distances. The internet is the biggest WAN.

IP Address

An IP address identifies a device on a network so data can reach the right destination.

Router

A router forwards data between networks and helps devices connect to the internet.

Switch

A switch connects devices inside a LAN and sends data to the correct device.

Important Network Protocols

Protocols are communication rules that help devices exchange data correctly.

TCP

Reliable protocol that checks whether data packets are delivered correctly.

UDP

Fast protocol with less checking, often used for gaming, calls, and streaming.

HTTP / HTTPS

Used for websites. HTTPS adds encryption to make communication safer.

DNS

Converts domain names like google.com into IP addresses used by computers.

How Data Travels in a Network

When you open a website or send a message, data moves in small units called packets.

1. Request: Your device sends a request, such as opening a webpage.
2. Packet Creation: The data is divided into small packets.
3. Routing: Routers choose paths for the packets.
4. Delivery: Packets reach the destination device or server.
5. Rebuild: The packets are arranged again to show the final page or message.
